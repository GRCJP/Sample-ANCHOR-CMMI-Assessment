SYSTEM ASSESSMENT REPORT
Maryland Department of Transportation (MDOT)
Assessment ID: MD-MDOT-2026-041  |  May 19, 2026
Prepared by: Assurit Consulting Group  |  CONFIDENTIAL
1. Executive Summary
Assurit Consulting Group conducted a comprehensive cybersecurity assessment of MDOT's enterprise IT environment from April 7 – May 14, 2026. The assessment applied the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 as the primary evaluation framework, with controls mapped to NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5. Maturity scoring utilized the CMMI 1–5 scale.

MDOT achieved an overall maturity score of 2.5 / 5.0, indicating a Managed level with significant opportunities to advance toward a Defined posture. Four (4) critical and nine (9) high-priority findings require immediate attention.

2. Scope
47 enterprise systems, 12 network segments, AWS GovCloud and Azure Government environments, 8 field offices, and 3 OT interfaces were within scope.

3. Methodology
Evidence was collected through documentation review, stakeholder interviews (6 conducted), automated scanning (Nessus, network mapping), and technical testing. All 106 NIST CSF 2.0 subcategories were assessed.

4. Key Findings Summary
• 4 Critical | 9 High | 7 Medium | 3 Low findings identified
• Lowest maturity: Detect (2.2), Recover (2.0)
• Strongest area: Protect (3.1) — existing access controls and training programs

5. Recommendations
Prioritized 23 POA&M items developed. Immediate actions (0–30 days) include SIEM deployment, MFA enforcement, and IR plan refresh.
