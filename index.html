CMMI Cybersecurity Assessment — Methodology Overview
Maryland Dept. of Transportation (MDOT)  ·  8-Slide Overview
Slide 1 — Welcome
Engagement Overview & Who We Are
  • Assurit Consulting Group — RFP BPM052516 awardee
  • Maryland DoIT statewide cybersecurity assessment program
  • ANCHOR Framework: structured delivery across ~90 agencies
  • Your engagement: MDOT Enterprise IT & OT infrastructure
  • Key Assurit contacts, pod composition, lead assessor
Slide 2 — The Mandate
Why This Assessment & Why CMMI
  • Maryland DoIT directive: standardized maturity across all state agencies
  • Federal alignment: FISMA, NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5, OMB M-22-09
  • CMMI 0–5 scale provides objective, repeatable scoring across agencies
  • Findings directly inform POA&M prioritization and budget planning
  • Results reported to DoIT and agency leadership — executive-ready
Slide 3 — Framework
NIST CSF 2.0 — What We Assess
  • 6 Functions: GOVERN · IDENTIFY · PROTECT · DETECT · RESPOND · RECOVER
  • Each function maps to NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 controls
  • Controls assessed via EXAMINE, INTERVIEW, and TEST methods
  • MDOT scope: enterprise IT, cloud (AWS/Azure), OT interfaces, 3rd-party vendors
  • Assets identified, prioritized by criticality and data classification
Slide 4 — Process
End-to-End Assessment Lifecycle
  • PRE-ASSESS: Intake, scoping, questionnaire, schedule confirmation
  • ASSESS: CSF workbook, stakeholder interviews, technical testing
  • NORMALIZE: Evidence review, data reconciliation, CMMI scoring
  • CONSOLIDATE: Gap analysis, risk register, finding validation
  • REPORT: SAR delivery, POA&M, executive briefing to MDOT & DoIT
Slide 5 — Your Role
Pre-Assessment Questionnaire & Your Responsibilities
  • Complete the pre-assessment questionnaire within 5–7 business days
  • Designate an agency POC for scheduling interviews (IT lead, CISO)
  • Identify system owners and stakeholders for each assessment area
  • Coordinate 24/7 operational constraints for interview scheduling
  • Primary contact: Lisa Harmon, CISO — l.harmon@mdot.maryland.gov
Slide 6 — Evidence
Artifact & Evidence Requirements
  • Security policies, procedures, incident response plans, BCP/DR
  • Network diagrams, asset inventories, system boundary documentation
  • Prior assessments, audit findings, POA&M history
  • Vulnerability scan results, patch management records
  • Secure upload via CyberAssess Agency Portal (role-based access)
Slide 7 — Scoring
CMMI Scoring & Risk Identification
  • 0 — Nonexistent  ·  1 — Performed  ·  2 — Managed
  • 3 — Defined  ·  4 — Measured  ·  5 — Optimized
  • Risks scored by likelihood, impact, and business criticality
  • Findings outside standard control maps flagged and reconciled
  • Consistent cross-agency scoring via standardized SRTM workbook
Slide 8 — Deliverables
What You Receive & Next Steps
  • SAR — System Assessment Report with full findings & maturity scores
  • POA&M — Prioritized remediation plan with assigned owners & timelines
  • Executive Brief — Tailored for MDOT leadership & DoIT audience
  • Kick-off: April 7, 2026  ·  SAR delivery: May 19, 2026
  • Questions? Contact J. Williams — lead assessor, Pod LARGE, Wave 1

Maryland Department of Transportation (MDOT)
Assessment ID: MD-MDOT-2026-041  |  May 19, 2026
Prepared by: Assurit Consulting Group  |  CONFIDENTIAL
1. Executive Summary
Assurit Consulting Group conducted a comprehensive cybersecurity assessment of MDOT's enterprise IT environment from April 7 – May 14, 2026. The assessment applied the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 as the primary evaluation framework, with controls mapped to NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5. Maturity scoring utilized the CMMI 1–5 scale.

MDOT achieved an overall maturity score of 2.5 / 5.0, indicating a Managed level with significant opportunities to advance toward a Defined posture. Four (4) critical and nine (9) high-priority findings require immediate attention.

2. Scope
47 enterprise systems, 12 network segments, AWS GovCloud and Azure Government environments, 8 field offices, and 3 OT interfaces were within scope.

3. Methodology
Evidence was collected through documentation review, stakeholder interviews (6 conducted), automated scanning (Nessus, network mapping), and technical testing. All 106 NIST CSF 2.0 subcategories were assessed.

4. Key Findings Summary
• 4 Critical | 9 High | 7 Medium | 3 Low findings identified
• Lowest maturity: Detect (2.2), Recover (2.0)
• Strongest area: Protect (3.1) — existing access controls and training programs

5. Recommendations
Prioritized 23 POA&M items developed. Immediate actions (0–30 days) include SIEM deployment, MFA enforcement, and IR plan refresh.
