Risk Register — Preliminary Findings
How Risk Scores Are Calculated
Findings are derived from controls scored NOT MET during the NIST CSF 2.0 assessment. Likelihood is derived from the control's CMMI maturity score — a lower CMMI level means weaker control implementation, which means higher probability of exploit. Impact reflects data sensitivity and mission criticality of the affected system. Score is calculated as:
Score = Likelihood Weight × Impact Weight

Step-by-Step Example: GV.RM-01 (Risk Management Not Documented)
1
Control Identified — GV.RM-01 scored NOT MET: no formal risk management policy or documented risk register at DPSCS
2
Evidence Reviewed — No approved risk management plan, no periodic risk assessments, no documented risk tolerance found in audit evidence package
3
CMMI Level Set — CMMI 1 (Initial): risk activities occur ad hoc with no repeatable, documented process
4
Agency Context — DPSCS manages correctional facility PII, CJIS criminal justice data, and inmate health records → data classified as Critical
5
Likelihood Assigned — CMMI 1 = weaker control = higher exploit probability → Likelihood: High (Weight = 4)
6
Impact Assigned — CJIS/PII/inmate health data exposure = Critical → Impact: Critical (Weight = 5)
7
Score Calculated — 4 × 5 = 20 → P1 Critical — escalated to POA&M with 30-day remediation target

Scope and Limitations
IN SCOPE

  • Identity and access management controls
  • Security monitoring and detection capabilities
  • Incident response policies and procedures

1. Executive Summary

Assurit Consulting Group conducted a comprehensive cybersecurity assessment of the Maryland Dept. of Public Safety & Correctional Services (DPSCS) enterprise IT environment. The assessment applied the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 as the primary evaluation framework, with controls mapped to NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5. Maturity scoring utilized the CMMI 0-5 scale.

DPSCS achieved an overall maturity score of 1.8 / 5.0, indicating a Performed maturity level. 18 control(s) were assessed as NOT MET, requiring remediation. 5 control(s) MET requirements. 23 total controls assessed across 6 CSF functions. Key challenges include integration of physical security systems with enterprise IT controls, legacy correctional management systems, and limited cybersecurity staffing relative to the agency's operational footprint across 25+ facilities statewide.

2. Scope & Methodology

The assessment evaluated DPSCS systems against all NIST CSF 2.0 functions: Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover. Evidence was collected through documentation review, stakeholder interviews (5 sessions recorded), automated scanning, technical testing, and physical site visits at 3 facilities. All 23 controls and their assessment procedures were evaluated using the EXAMINE / INTERVIEW / TEST methodology aligned with the SRTM format. Assessment period: March 10 – June 14, 2026.

7. Recommendations

• IMMEDIATE (0–30 days): Address all CRITICAL findings — deploy SIEM, begin physical/logical IAM integration, update legacy system inventory.

• SHORT-TERM (30–90 days): Update cybersecurity policy; revise and exercise incident response plan with cyber-physical scenarios.

• MEDIUM-TERM (90–180 days): Complete mandatory security awareness training; develop TPRM framework for correctional technology vendors.

• ONGOING: Establish continuous monitoring across all facility networks; conduct quarterly vulnerability assessments; track POA&M progress monthly with facility ISOs.
