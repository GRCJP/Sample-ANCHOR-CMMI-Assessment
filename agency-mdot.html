How Risk Scores Are Calculated
Findings are derived from controls scored NOT MET during the NIST CSF 2.0 assessment. Likelihood is derived from the control's CMMI maturity score — a lower CMMI level means weaker control implementation, which means higher probability of exploit. Impact reflects data sensitivity and mission criticality of the affected system. Score is calculated as:
Score = Likelihood Weight × Impact Weight

Step-by-Step Example: DE.CM-01 (No SIEM / Continuous Monitoring)
1
Control Identified — DE.CM-01 scored NOT MET: no SIEM deployed, no continuous monitoring capability at MDOT
2
Evidence Reviewed — No centralized log aggregation, no alerting rules, no SOC coverage or monitoring runbooks found in audit evidence package
3
CMMI Level Set — CMMI 1 (Initial): need was recognized but no formal documented process or tooling in place
4
Agency Context — MDOT manages transportation infrastructure and driver license PII → data classified as Critical
5
Likelihood Assigned — CMMI 1 = weaker control = higher exploit probability → Likelihood: High (Weight = 4)
6
Impact Assigned — Transportation infrastructure + driver license PII exposure = Critical → Impact: Critical (Weight = 5)
7
Score Calculated — 4 × 5 = 20 → P1 Critical — escalated to POA&M with 30-day remediation target
Scoring validated by FRTA senior reviewer before delivery to OSM.

Assessment Framework
NIST CSF 2.0
Primary assessment framework. Evaluates cybersecurity posture across six functions: Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover.
ANCHOR CMMI Overlay
5-level maturity scoring (0=Non-Existent to 5=Optimized) applied per control to measure implementation depth and consistency.
📄
EXAMINE
Policy review, configuration inspection, system documentation, logs and reports analysis
💬
INTERVIEW
Structured interviews with CISO, IT leadership, system owners, and operational staff
🔍
TEST
Technical verification, configuration validation, scan review, and process walkthrough
Scope and Limitations
IN SCOPE
  • Enterprise network infrastructure and systems
  • Identity and access management controls
  • Security monitoring and detection capabilities
  • Incident response policies and procedures
  • Vendor/third-party risk management practices
  • Patch management and vulnerability processes
LIMITATIONS
  • Assessment based on documentation and interviews; not a penetration test
  • Cloud-hosted applications assessed at policy level only
  • OT/ICS systems outside assessment boundary
  • Point-in-time assessment — conditions may change
