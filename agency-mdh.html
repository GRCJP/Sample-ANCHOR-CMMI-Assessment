1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Maryland Department of Information Technology (DoIT), Office of Security Management, conducted a formal cybersecurity maturity assessment of the Maryland Department of Health (MDH) pursuant to the State IT Security Policy (DOIT-ITRM-POLICY-001). This assessment utilized the ANCHOR Platform framework aligned to NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 and Capability Maturity Model Integration (CMMI) maturity level scoring.

MDH demonstrated a current overall cybersecurity maturity of ML 2.1 (Managed) across 20 assessed controls spanning six CSF 2.0 functions. The agency processes sensitive healthcare data subject to HIPAA requirements and has established foundational security policies; however, significant gaps exist in HIPAA-aligned data protection, PHI access controls, continuous monitoring, and third-party healthcare vendor risk governance.

MDH is directed to remediate all Critical and High findings within the timeframes specified in the accompanying Plan of Action & Milestones (POA&M) and achieve a target maturity of ML 3.0 (Defined) within 12 months of this report date.
